## How this lab works

These simulations **measure** quantities for you — but they do **not** do the physics.
For every trial you must **calculate** the required values yourself using the equations
from the lab module, then type your answers into the **Your Calculation** panel:

1. ✅ Press **Check my answers** — each value is marked *correct*, *too high* or *too low*.
2. 🔓 Only after checking can you **reveal the exact values** to compare with your own.
3. 📋 Every trial is logged in the **Trial History** table — use **⬇ Download CSV** to
   export your data for your lab report.

> Keep your signs consistent: rightward/downward speeds as defined in each experiment,
> and remember g = 9.8 m/s².

## Experiment 1 — Momentum and Impulsive Force (Egg Drop)

**Page:** [index.html](https://mkhalis85.github.io/phy3901-virtual-lab/) (the home page)

1. Select the **egg size** (small / large / jumbo), **drop height** (1–10 m) and
   **landing surface** (hard floor / 1-inch foam / foam box). The selected
   conditions are highlighted in **red**.
2. Press **▼ Drop Egg** and watch the fall and impact.
3. The Trial Report gives the **measured data only**: mass *m*, drop height *h*,
   time of impact *t*, and the egg's final condition (intact / fractured / broken).
4. **Calculate** the change in velocity (Δv = √(2gh), since the egg stops on landing)
   and the impulsive force (F = mΔv⁄t), then check your answers.
5. Repeat to complete **Table 1** of the lab module:
   - **Set A** — vary drop height (1 m, 5 m, 10 m); small egg, hard floor
   - **Set B** — vary egg size; 1 m, hard floor
   - **Set C** — vary surface; small egg, 1 m
6. Explore other combinations to answer the assignment questions.

## Experiment 2 — Conservation of Momentum (1D Collisions)

**Page:** [momentum.html](https://mkhalis85.github.io/phy3901-virtual-lab/momentum.html)

Two boxes slide toward each other on a frictionless surface (rightward velocity is positive).

1. Set the **masses** and **initial velocities** of the red and blue boxes with the sliders.
2. Set the **elasticity**: use the preset buttons **e = 0.5** (inelastic),
   **e = 1.0** (elastic) and **e = 0.0** (perfectly inelastic) as required by the module.
3. Press **▶ Run** (you can **❚❚ Pause** and **Reset** at any time).
4. After the collision, the Measured Data panel records the **final velocities** v₁ and v₂.
5. **Calculate** p = mv and KE = ½mv² for each box, before and after the collision,
   and the four totals (Σp before/after, ΣKE before/after) — then check your totals.
6. For **each elasticity value**, record **three data sets** with different masses and
   velocities, as required for **Table 2** (a), (b), (c).

## Experiment 3 — Ballistic Pendulum

**Page:** [pendulum.html](https://mkhalis85.github.io/phy3901-virtual-lab/pendulum.html)

A bullet is fired into a hanging wooden block; the bullet embeds (perfectly inelastic
collision) and the system swings upward. The bullet's initial velocity shows **???** —
your task is to determine it.

1. Make sure the **red reference line** is at the bottom of the block — **h = 0 cm**.
2. Choose the **bullet mass**, **block mass** and (unknown) **bullet velocity** sliders.
3. Press **⚡ Fire** and watch the block swing.
4. Move the **reference line** slider up until the dashed line just touches the
   **lowest edge of the block at the top of its swing**. The readout is your
   maximum height *h*. (Tick **slow motion ×¼** to make alignment easier.)
5. **Calculate** the bullet's initial velocity using conservation of momentum and energy:
   - Swing: v = √(2gh)
   - Collision: u = (m + M)·v ⁄ m
6. Enter your estimated *u* and press **Check my answer**.
7. Then tick **Show initial bullet velocity** to reveal the exact value and your
   percentage error — the trial is recorded for **Table 3**.
8. Repeat for **three trials** with different values of all three sliders.
